# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Productivity enthusiasts who are frustrated by tool overload — juggling Notion for notes, Trello for tasks, Habitica for habits, and Google Calendar for dates. Spanish-language-first audience. They want their work to feel coherent and calm, not scattered across five apps. They value simplicity but won't accept dumbness; they want power that doesn't require setup.

The landing page (`/`) is a **brand** surface — treat it with brand register when working on it. The app shell (`/app/*`) is **product** register. Both share the same visual language but differ in intent.

## Product Purpose

Rumbo is an all-in-one personal workspace: tasks (Kanban), notes (rich text), habits (daily tracking), calendar (monthly view), and Pomodoro timer — in one coherent, opinionated environment. Success means a user opens Rumbo in the morning and closes it at night without switching apps. Nothing to configure, nothing to template-browse.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, clear, purposeful — but alive. The foundation is restraint (Bear / Apple clarity), the finish is energy (expressive motion, intentional eye-catching detail). Not flashy, not flat. Still, precise, then surprising.

## Anti-references

- **Bear App / Apple native UI**: too static, too conservative, too expected. Rumbo inherits their clarity but not their inertness.
- **Notion**: blocky, text-heavy, template-forest, requires onboarding.
- **Todoist / TickTick**: dense, list-maximalist, utilitarian to a fault.
- **Trello**: visual noise, color overload, no sense of flow.
- **Generic SaaS / startup blue**: navy gradients, hero-metric grids, team-collaboration pitch.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity first, energy as the finish.** Every layout decision starts from restraint. Motion and visual interest are layered on top — never instead of — structure.
2. **The workspace works immediately.** No empty states requiring setup. No template pickers. Open Rumbo and work.
3. **Motion communicates, not decorates.** Animations should carry meaning: what appeared, what changed, what's available. Rumbo invests more in motion than Bear or Apple defaults — every transition should feel earned and deliberate.
4. **One visual language across all views.** Switching from Board to Habits to Notes should feel like walking between rooms in the same building, not teleporting to a different app.
5. **Respect focus.** UI elements should recede when not in use. The user's content is the loudest thing on screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the baseline. Full keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations (`prefers-reduced-motion`). Sufficient contrast for body text (≥4.5:1) and large text (≥3:1). Dark mode fully supported.
